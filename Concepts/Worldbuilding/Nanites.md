Concept 1:

Obviously not every nanite can create every material. Nanites are still limited by physical laws.

The reason why nanites work is because of the discovery of a fictional element called Morpheus (temp). Morpheus itself does nothing, but when current runs through it, it oxidizes on the surface, taking whatever kind of molecules and electrons needed to create a specific material, based on current, size of the Morpheus chunk and the surrounding temperature. All this combined with nano technology and circuits allows us to send controlled currents to microscopic chunks of Morpheus, being able to have them grow in a predefined way. The main constraint here is time. If the material charges up too fast or the temperature fluctuates, the entire structure can become unstable. Very specific amounts of Morpheus can even reproduce themselves, but that is a extremely difficult treat only found in very few constellations of Morpheus and uranium.
